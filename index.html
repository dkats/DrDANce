Kids are not just little versions of adults, so they have specific needs for physical activity. Their bodies are growing and developing, and adult exercise and conditioning programs are not meant for them. High stress or continuous repetitive movements should be done with extra caution, as this kind of exercise can cause injury. In other words, dancing and other complex exercises are better than a pure "workout" routine. (American College of Sports Medicine)

Sworkit

A 2015 study at the University of Florida, identified Sworkit as most closely following the guidelines set by the American College of Sports Medicine out of a pool of 30 apps. I personally use* Sworkit and am very happy with it. The best part about it is that it allows for incredibly flexible workouts: they're all designed to be done without any equipment (although, there are also options for dumbbells and elastic bands) and for any amount of time.

*I do not have a conflict of interest with them; they do not pay me or give me a discount. I'm mentioning them because I genuinely like the product. I will disclose, however, that they gave me a coupon code for my patients to get 40% for unlimited years. I do not get a kick-back if my patients sign up.

Here are my thoughts about the free version (disclaimer: last time I used the free version was a few years ago, so it may have changed since then):

PROs:

  • Very easy to use
  • Can pick any duration of exercise
  • Exercises are very simple, and don't require any equipment
  • Short video clips show exactly what to do for each exercise
  • Each exercise only lasts 30 seconds, there are 30 second breaks sprinkled in
  • Completely free! There is a premium version that gives you a little extra, but it's absolutely unnecessary

CONs:

  • Pool of exercises is relatively small and can be repetitive. There are usually enough to avoid repeats during a 10-minute workout.
  • The exercises only have videos, not specific instructions, so you have to figure out how to get the most out of it. For example, for a "swimmer" (lying on your belly and moving your arms and legs up and down as if you're swimming), it's up to you to figure out how high to raise your arms and legs, how quickly to move, etc. The videos are pretty helpful, though.

For the paid version, there's an enormous pool of exercises and they have lots of premade workouts available. Personally, I use custom workouts that I made myself, but I've tried the premade ones and was pretty happy with them. The only reason I don't use them more often is because they don't cater to an injury that I have.

They also have a Youth Initiative, in which they offer the kids apps (iOS | Android) for free. On top of that, teachers and school administrators can fill out a form to get the paid version of Sworkit for free! The kids apps are meant for elementary schoolers, while the other apps are for older kids (and adults).

  • Ab & Core: iOS
